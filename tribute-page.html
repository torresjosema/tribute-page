H. P. Lovecraft timeline biography:

  • Aug 1890-Born in Providence, Rhode Island.
  • 1893-When hi was three year old, his father was placed in Butler Hospital due he suffered a nervous breakdown caused by an untreated syphilis. He dies in 1898.
  • 1897-Continuing to study at home, Howard wrote his first poem, "The poem of Ulysses".
  • 1898-At the age of eight, he was introduced to chemistry and astronomy, dreaming to become an astronaut when he grew up. It was also the time, when he started growing a fondness for the writings of Edgar Allen Poe.
  • 1899-Plagued by various ailments like headache, nervousness and general fatigue, he had to leave school. He produced "The Scientific Gazette".
  • 1903-After studing at home for four years, returned to school, getting admission into Hope High School. He started "The Rhode Island Journal of Astronomy", like "The Scientific Gazette" it also survived till 1907.
  • 1904-His maternal grandfather passed away all of a sudden and their financial condition began to deteriorate.
  • 1905-Young Howard found it very hard to accept such degradation of their status, contemplating suicide. But very soon he got out of his mood and began normal life, writing "The Beast in the Cave".
  • 1906-His letter on certain astronomical subject appeared in "The Providence Sunday Journal". Very soon, he started writing a monthly astronomy column for different journals.
  • 1908-Shortly before his final examination, he suffered a nervous breakdown, wich compelled him to leave school without a diploma. It affected him so much that he went into reclusion.
  • 1913-A chance incident led him out of his seclusion, helping him to take up writing as his career. Some Fred Jackson wrote a series of insipid love stories in a pulp magazine called "Argosy". Reading them, he became so infuriated that he wrote a letter attacking Jackson.
  • 1914-Lovecraft joined UAPA on the invitation of Daas, launching his own paper, "The Conservative" in 1915.
  • 1916-Howard submitted "The Alchemist", a short tory he had written in 1908 before he entered his reclusion, to "United Amateur". It was his first published short story.
  • 1922-Encouraged by Cook, Lovevecraft began to write fictions, producing "The Tomb" and "Dagon". Thereafter, he produced number of short stories althoght until 1922 poems and essays remained his preferred mode of literary expression.
  • Feb 1924-He was commissioned by J.C. Henneberger, founder and owner of "Weird Tales", to ghost-write a story for magician Harry Houdini and was offered $100 for it.
  • March 1924-Lovecraft got married and relocated to Brooklyn. After detailed research on pharaohs, he wrote "Imprisioned with the Pharaohs".
  • 1926-Altough intially he enjoyed life in New York the good times did not last long. Very soon he started facing financial problems and dsicord at home. Eventually on April 17, he returned to Providence without his wife where he spent the last years of his life, producing a vast amount of celebrated work. "The call of Cthulhu" is one of his most memorable works.
  • 1927-He wrote a short novel called "The case of Charles Dexter Ward". However, he himself found it "cumbrous, creaking bit of self-conscious antiquarianism" an therefore left it to be published posthumously.
  • 1928-Howard wrote "Downwich Horror".
  • 1931-Lovecraft wrote "At the Mountains of Madness" and "The Shadow over Innsmouth".
  • 1934-1935-He wrote "The shadow out of Time".
  • 1936-"The Shadow over Innsmouth" was published. This novel is his only creation publishad in book for during his lifetime.
  • 1937-Lovecraft was diagnosed with cancer of the small intestine. On March 10, he was admitted on Jane Brown Memorial Hospital in Providence and died there on March 15.
